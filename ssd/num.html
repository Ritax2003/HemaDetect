Know Your Parameters

Detect a Pulsar Using the Following parameters:
Integrated Profile: It represents the average intensity of the pulsar's signal over a period of observation, typically summed up and normalized.

  • Mean: Average intensity of the profile
  • Standard Deviation: Measures the spread or variability of intensity values
  • Excess Kurtosis: Indicates "tailedness" of the distribution of intensity values
  • Skewness: Measures the asymmetry of the intensity distribution

    • DM-SNR Curve: The DM-SNR curve is a plot that shows the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) as a function of the dispersion measure (DM).

  • Mean: Average SNR value across different DM values
  • Standard Deviation: Measures variability of SNR values across different DMs
  • Excess Kurtosis: Indicates the shape or "tailedness" of the SNR distribution
  • Skewness: Measures the asymmetry of the SNR distribution
